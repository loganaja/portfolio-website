THE OBJECTIVE

The existing robotic loading and unloading frames required a comprehensive structural and functional overhaul to reduce system complexity, lower costs, and introduce true modular adaptability for varying factory floor layouts. I was tasked with leading the electromechanical optimization, improving the glass barrel dispensing sub-system, and refining the underlying robotic motion control scripts.

THE EXECUTION

I performed a thorough design audit to strip out redundant components, generated an optimized Bill of Materials (BOM) for the electrical and pneumatic infrastructure, and integrated dedicated cable management and illumination fixtures. For the glass barrel dispenser, I engineered a new hopper adjustment mechanism, a low-friction Teflon gate, and a high-precision blade alignment system. On the software side, I developed and refined the system's Python control code—implementing advanced homing routines, safety protocols, adaptive unloading algorithms, and synchronized waiting functions to optimize robot duty cycles. Additionally, I managed the end-to-end procurement pipeline and supplier tracking for all large-scale components.

THE IMPACT

The newly modularized frames significantly cut down future engineering redesign cycles, directly boosted robotic motion efficiency, and drastically improved the reliability and throughput of the dispensing mechanism.
