THE OBJECTIVE

The Lapping 5 production-grade manufacturing machine required full mechanical assembly, field wiring, and critical engineering optimizations to meet a strict shipment schedule. Working under the guidance of senior engineers and electricians, I was tasked with executing the electromechanical build while identifying and correcting high-risk failure points in the system's indexing mechanism.

THE EXECUTION

I routed and structured all field cables and pneumatic airlines across six complex modules, interpreting electrical schematics to organize and label infrastructure for rapid troubleshooting. Addressing high-impact mechanical issues, I researched and redesigned the machine indexer using aerospace-grade aluminum to eliminate thermal warping and prevent system crashes. Furthermore, I resolved critical fluid leaks, fabricated custom protective covers, integrated a 3D-printed reject tray, performed CAD updates for the indexer motor assembly, and directly programmed the chipping robot while optimizing the line's automated vision inspection camera system.

THE IMPACT

The Lapping 5 machine was successfully commissioned and delivered on schedule, significantly reducing mechanical failure rates and sharpening my expertise in full-scale machine production, testing, and industrial integration.
