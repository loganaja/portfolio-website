THE OBJECTIVE

Following the successful validation of the Mk1 proof-of-concept, I was tasked with developing the Mk2 prototype to bridge the gap between initial testing and a production-ready unit. The primary objective was to design a semi-portable, enclosed system capable of failure-analysis research while strictly minimizing manufacturing costs.

THE EXECUTION

I engineered a complete CAD model of the unit utilizing cost-effective aluminum extrusions and repurposed acrylic sheets shaped via laser cutting. To maintain a low budget, I 3D-printed custom mechanical components including hinges, handles, and LED mounts. Additionally, I modified the mounting framework to accommodate a new stepper motor and integrated a standalone electrical cabinet using salvaged components to evaluate system portability and component longevity.

THE IMPACT

The Mk2 prototype successfully served as an active R&D research platform, providing critical mechanical wear data that identified component failure points and informed the final production design.
