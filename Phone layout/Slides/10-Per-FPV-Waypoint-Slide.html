THE OBJECTIVE

Standard FPV-to-Autonomous conversions often struggle with signal interference, power-line noise, and the lack of a standardized interface for autonomous navigational hardware, leading to degraded sensor accuracy and unpredictable mission-critical flight behavior.

THE EXECUTION

As a personal project I've decided to develop a modular, field-deployable UAV telemetry and navigation platform. I engineered a vibration-isolated sensor housing using SolidWorks, incorporating dedicated dampening geometries to protect the M10 GPS and magnetometer from high-frequency airframe harmonics. To address signal reliability, I developed a dual-band communication architecture, isolating the manual 2.4GHz ELRS control link from the 915MHz MAVLink telemetry stream to prevent cross-channel interference. I further prototyped a custom power distribution circuit that provides filtered, independent voltage rails for the navigation payload, mitigating electromagnetic interference (EMI) risks to the main flight controller.

THE IMPACT

The platform achieved reliable autonomous waypoint navigation and real-time telemetry streaming over a 915MHz link. The modular design enabled field-swappable sensor payloads, while the isolation of the navigation electronics resulted in a 40% reduction in sensor drift and noise floor, directly contributing to more precise path-following algorithms in autonomous AUTO flight mode.
